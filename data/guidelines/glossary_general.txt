# General Medical Glossary (cross-specialty)

Neutropenic fever: Fever in a patient with low neutrophils; treat as emergency infection.
AKI: Acute kidney injury; sudden loss of kidney function.
CKD: Chronic kidney disease.
Hematuria: Blood in urine.
Thrombocytopenia: Low platelets.
Leukocytosis: High white blood cell count.
Leukopenia: Low white blood cell count.
Hyponatremia: Low sodium.
Hyperkalemia: High potassium.
Hypokalemia: Low potassium.
Pneumonia: Lung infection.
CAP: Community-acquired pneumonia.
UTI: Urinary tract infection.
Sepsis: Life-threatening organ dysfunction from infection.
Neutrophils: Type of white blood cell; critical for fighting infection.
INR: Clotting measure; high INR = bleeding risk.
Anticoagulant: Blood thinner (e.g., heparin, warfarin).
Antiplatelet: Drug that stops platelets (e.g., aspirin, clopidogrel).
Hypotension: Low blood pressure.
Tachycardia: High heart rate.
Bradycardia: Low heart rate.
Dyspnea: Shortness of breath.
Hypoxia: Low oxygen saturation.
