# ACS Glossary (plain-language)

STEMI: ST-segment elevation myocardial infarction (full blockage)
NSTEMI: Non-ST-elevation myocardial infarction (partial blockage)
Unstable angina: Chest pain at rest or increasing, no troponin rise
Retrosternal pain: Central chest pain, pressure-like, may radiate to arm/jaw
Red flags: Hypotension, new ST elevation, cardiogenic shock, arrhythmia, altered mental status
First-line ACS meds: Aspirin, clopidogrel, nitroglycerin, beta-blocker (if no shock), statin, anticoagulant
Do not give: Beta-blocker in shock/bradycardia, nitrates in right ventricular infarct or hypotension
Time-sensitive: Door-to-balloon within 90 minutes (PCI) or thrombolysis if PCI unavailable
ECG changes: ST elevation, new LBBB (treat as STEMI), ST depression/T inversion (possible NSTEMI/UA)
Troponin: Cardiac enzyme to confirm MI; repeat if initial negative
